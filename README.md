#  AI-Powered Gold Price Predictor

An interactive, self-updating, and production-ready web application that leverages **Meta's Prophet AI model** to forecast global gold prices. The app automatically fetches live market data using the Yahoo Finance API, trains the machine learning model on-the-fly, and calculates the predicted prices for different gold karats (24K, 22K, 21K, and 18K) on any future date selected by the user.

##  How the AI Model Works & Predicts Accurately

Predicting gold prices is highly complex. Unlike simple linear regression which assumes a straight-line growth, gold prices fluctuate based on macroeconomic cycles, weekends, and annual seasons. 

To achieve high accuracy, this model uses **Meta's Prophet**, which treats forecasting as a curve-fitting exercise. The model calculates predictions based on three key components:

**Overall Forcast Plot:**
<img width="996" height="563" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/102d2625-31fe-49ab-bb3c-a66278907271" />


### 1. Trend Component $g(t)$
It detects the "long-term direction" of gold. The model analyzes historical data to see if gold is generally gaining value over the years (due to inflation, geopolitics, etc.). Prophet is smart enough to identify **Changepoints**—specific dates where the trend trajectory suddenly changed (e.g., during major economic crises).
<img width="912" height="271" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8810bb72-b708-4f31-9080-36e46baaa7aa" />


### 2. Weekly Seasonality $s_{\text{weekly}}(t)$
Gold is traded on global exchanges which **close on weekends** (Saturday and Sunday). Prophet automatically models this weekly cycle, understanding that trading volume and price movements behave differently on Mondays compared to Fridays.

<img width="930" height="272" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/88c45647-1060-4033-aa33-9658a7975126" />


### 3. Yearly Seasonality $s_{\text{yearly}}(t)$
Gold historically experiences strong seasonal demand. For instance, prices often spike during certain times of the year (such as Q4 holiday seasons, global festival peaks, or wedding seasons). The model learns these recurring annual patterns and factor them into future predictions.
